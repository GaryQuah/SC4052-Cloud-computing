Key Discussion Points
1. Recruitment Pipeline
Kevin Tan reported that there are currently 8 open positions across Engineering, Sales, and Marketing.
He mentioned that 3 candidates have been shortlisted for Engineering roles, with interviews scheduled for the following week.
Kevin Tan added that the recruitment team is exploring new job boards and platforms, particularly those targeting diverse talent pools, to improve candidate quality and inclusivity.
Amanda Chen asked about time-to-hire metrics, and Ravi Kumar noted that the average hiring cycle has increased slightly due to more rigorous screening processes.
2. Onboarding & Training
Emily Lim shared that the new onboarding schedule for April hires has been finalized, with a structured 2-week program covering company orientation, tools training, and team introductions.
She mentioned that a training program focused on soft skills and compliance (e.g., workplace ethics, data protection) will be rolled out next month.
Lisa Wong highlighted that feedback from previous onboarding sessions showed high satisfaction overall, but employees requested more role-specific mentorship and hands-on guidance.
Amanda Chen suggested assigning team mentors or buddies to new hires to improve integration.
3. HR Policy Review
Amanda Chen led the discussion on annual leave policy updates, confirming that proposed changes have been approved and will be communicated via email to all staff.
Ravi Kumar noted that the updates aim to provide greater flexibility in leave planning while maintaining operational coverage.
The team also finalized revisions to the remote work policy, supporting a hybrid work arrangement with clearer guidelines on in-office expectations and remote eligibility.
Kevin Tan raised a concern about consistency across departments, and Amanda agreed to provide standardized guidelines.
4. Employee Engagement
Lisa Wong shared plans for a quarterly virtual team-building event scheduled for early May, aimed at improving cross-team collaboration.
She mentioned that recent employee survey results indicate strong interest in wellness programs, such as mental health initiatives and fitness activities.
Ravi Kumar added that employees also expressed a preference for more flexible working hours, especially for teams working across different time zones.
Amanda Chen suggested piloting a flexible hours framework in selected departments before company-wide adoption.
5. Other Business
The team discussed implementing an internal referral bonus scheme to encourage employees to recommend qualified candidates.
Kevin Tan suggested developing a hiring tracker dashboard for management to monitor recruitment progress, candidate stages, and hiring metrics in real time.
Ravi Kumar offered to support this by providing data analytics and reporting structures.
Emily Lim briefly mentioned the possibility of aligning onboarding content with recruitment insights to ensure smoother transitions for new hires.
Action Items
Kevin Tan to proceed with Engineering candidate interviews and explore additional recruitment platforms.
Emily Lim to finalize onboarding materials and prepare training rollout for next month.
Amanda Chen to communicate updated HR policies and define hybrid work guidelines.
Lisa Wong to organize the May team-building event and propose wellness initiatives.
Ravi Kumar to support development of the hiring tracker dashboard and provide hiring analytics.
